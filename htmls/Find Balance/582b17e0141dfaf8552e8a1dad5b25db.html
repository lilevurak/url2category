5 Strategies to Reduce Stress at Work (or Anywhere Else)

/in Blog /by tamon

It’s no secret that stress affects most people – and for most, that impact is negative. Beyond the emotional state of anxiety, pressure, or worry, stress can have a big impact on your health.

 

“Individuals who complain of ‘feeling stressed out’ oftentimes present with a myriad of complaints including chest pain, shortness of breath, headaches, and abdominal pain,” says Dr. Theresa Watts, medical director for OurHealth’s Charlotte MyClinic network.

And, according to the Mayo Clinic, long-term stress and its associated elevated levels of cortisol and other stress related hormones can lead to a higher risk for a whole host of conditions including heart disease, digestive problems, headaches, memory or concentration impairment, and other conditions.

If you’re stressed out – at home, at work, or anywhere else – try these five strategies for reducing the impact to your health:

No. 1 – Eat right, get plenty of exercise and get good sleep

These three activities go hand-in-hand and offer some powerful protection against stress. With proper nutrition, you’re giving your body good fuel, including antioxidant rich foods, which can counteract the cellular damage that stress can cause. Exercise is an excellent stress reliever because it stimulates the body to release endorphins, serotonin and dopamine—the feel good hormone. Physical activity also promotes deeper, more restful sleep – helping you feel more restored and less fatigued.

No. 2 – Practice relaxation with deep breathing, meditation or Yoga

Stress can affect your health by increasing blood pressure, heart rate, and decreasing blood oxygen levels. Deep breathing on its own, or through meditation or yoga can slow your heart rate, decrease blood pressure, and increase blood oxygen levels. Practicing meditation can also bestow the benefits of greater focus, improved mood, and better concentration. Finally, along with also promoting deep breathing, yoga’s deep stretching exercises can help relieve muscle tension and increase blood flow to your extremities.

No.3 – Practice positivity

Stress can often feel like an automatic reaction to an external stimulus. But how you react, interpret, and think about the causes of stress can be an important stress-reduction strategy. Practicing positive thinking and reframing your interpretation can help you pre-emptively cope with stress.

Here are three steps for practicing positive thinking when you encounter stress:

  1. Recognize when you feel stressed – Actively identify stress when it occurs. Awareness is always the first step to change.
  2. Reframe the situation – A challenging situation can be a difficult, stressful experience or an opportunity for growth – depending on how you frame the situation. Look for a fresh perspective to help you manage your stress.
  3. Focus on what you can control – There are always forces and factors beyond our control. Focusing on what you can control and accepting that you can’t control everything, can help you combat stress.

No. 4 – Laugh

Laughter is good medicine and an especially effective stress management strategy s. Laughing helps lower levels of cortisol, adrenaline and other stress related hormones. It also releases “feel good” hormones such as dopamine and increases your body’s intake of oxygen.

No. 5 – Make time for you and things you enjoy

One of the easiest ways to deal with stress? Do something you enjoy. Give yourself a break from what’s causing the stress with an enjoyable hobby, activity or diversion. Stress management efforts are always more effective when you surround yourself with friends and family that support you.
