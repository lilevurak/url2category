OPINION: Students need to spend more time developing healthy habits

  • Jordan Meaker | Contributor
  • Feb 15, 2017
  • 0

According to researchers, it can take an average of 66 days or more to form a new habit and even longer if one chooses a more difficult habit to adopt. For college students, theres no time better than the present to start picking up healthy habits. Learning to live a healthy lifestyle now is a habit that will stick with you even after you graduate.

College is a time for having new experiences and learning life skills that are necessary for life in the real world, so students should use this opportunity to explore different lifestyle choices and see what fits best.

There are more forms of exercise than just running or using gym equipment. Club sports, fitness clubs and classes at Ramsey let students try a variety of different types of exercise to find what works best and is the most satisfying for them. For example, clubs on like CHAARG encourage students to find the type of exercise that they enjoy the most.

Additionally, many students decide to live off campus after their first year in college and many also leave the meal plan behind. Students can use this opportunity to start cooking for themselves for the first time. Studies have found that people who cook at home generally eat healthier and consume less calories that those that eat out often.

Another lifestyle habit that would be beneficial to pick up in college is learning how to handle a stressful schedule. Developing this habit now ensures students will have the necessary tools to handle future demanding careers.

Too much stress can also lead to negative health effects like high blood pressure, heart disease and stomach ulcers. He suggests stress management techniques like meditation, regular moderate exercise and getting enough sleep.

As young adults, our bodies and minds are still changing and evolving every year. Researchers at Northwestern University say that lifestyle choices that twenty year olds adopt can impact heart health later in life. The study indicated that leading a healthy life at a young age connected to a lower risk for cardiovascular disease once the participants reached their forties.

Developing healthy lifestyle practices during your college years can mitigate health problems that many adults face later in life. Even though college students face busy schedules, they must take time to develop beneficial habits that lay the foundation for a wholesome, well-rounded and successful adulthood.
