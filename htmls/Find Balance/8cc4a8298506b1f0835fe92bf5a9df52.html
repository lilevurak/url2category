New Year New You: Live a Heart-Healthy Lifestyle

Posted: Jan 16, 2017 06:48 PM EST

Updated: Jan 17, 2017 11:55 PM EST

SAYRE, Pa. (18 NEWS) - - With heart disease being the number one killer of men and women in the United States, doctors at Guthrie's Cardiovascular Center are urging people to become more heart-healthy this year.

"The disabilities, even if someone doesn't die after suffering numerous heart attacks, can be significant," Dr. Daniel Sporn said.

Although family history plays a big role in whether or not you could suffer from heart disease, Dr. Sporn says there are also a number of controllable risk factors.

"Patients or people that have family history of heart disease, meaning someone in their family had a heart attack at a younger age in their 40s or 50s, are going to be at higher risk," Dr. Sporn said. "The other people are people who have controllable risk factors... people that smoke cigarettes, people that have high cholesterol, high blood pressure, or diabetes are more likely to develop even other vascular problems."

The symptoms of heart disease can vary between men and women, but should never be taken lightly.

"[The] most feared complication would be something like a heart attack," Dr. Sporn said. "The symptoms for a heart attack can be the typical ones... chest discomfort, which people usually describe as a heaviness or tightness that may radiate down the left arm. Women can be different. They may not have those classic symptoms; they may just feel short of breath, they may feel tired, or they may have nausea. So some of these more big symptoms, we have to be on the lookout for as well."

Although the effects of heart disease can be deadly, doctors say there are some simple things you can do right now to lower your risk.

"Most of what causes heart attacks are reversible risk factors so if we could just get people to live a little bit healthier lifestyle most of this would go away," Dr. Sporn said.

Eating better and getting exercise could make all the difference.

"One of the most important things is to exercise and watch one's diet," Dr. Sporn said. "The diet is important in controlling blood pressure control and cholesterol and so in general, the American Heart Association recommends a low fat low-calorie and low cholesterol diet, and exercise is extremely important. According to the American Heart Association, they talk about people exercising at least 150 minutes per week, 2 1/2 hours per week, but ideally a little bit more is even better."

For those who have suffered from a cardiac event, treatment and prevention is just as important afterwards.

"Guthrie offers a very extensive program of cardiac rehab and then it's modifying the risk factors," Dr. Sporn said. "Things like blood pressure, if that's not in control you may want to get that lowered. For people that have elevated cholesterol, in addition to the diet, we may put them on medication. Certainly if they smoke, we will do everything we can to work with them to quit smoking and then again diet and exercise."

In the next New Year, New You segment, 18 News' Samantha Lantz will take a look at eye health and sharing why making an appointment with your optometrist could be the most important thing you do this year.
