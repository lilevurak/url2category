        That reminds me of a quote I just read by Dale Carnegie: “The load of tomorrow, added to that of yesterday, carried today, makes the strongest falter.” He also speaks of living our lives in what he calls “day-tight compartments,” meaning that we do not allow the past or the future to intrude, we just do the right thing now and trust that the future will be taking care of itself. This is something I am practicing, too. I am learning.

        It’s amazing, really. When you think about when this book was published (1949) and that it took seven years to write, that means that Dale Carnegie was writing this book during the height of WW II. Their world then was facing much the same things we are now, and worry was rampant. That is why so many wonderful screwball comedies came from the time around the Depression and the World Wars — people were stressed beyond imagination and needed a respite, if only for 90 minutes or so to watch Charlie Chaplin, Cary Grant, Clark Gable, or one of the many glamorous starlets who were the leading ladies of the day.

        And they worried about the future, too, just like us, and likely fought to stay grateful. I guess there really is nothing new under the sun! 😉

          I love this! What great inspiration and insights! Thank you for your thoughtfulness. Day tight compartments I’m trying to practice this as well. I guess we call it being in the moment or staying aware in the present moment. Mindfulness is also another term for day tight compartments.
          This is a very challenging practice because we are constantly bombarded by our minds distracting us back and forth from past to future, coupled with obviously the incredible amount of news we get in today’s fast passed world!
          I would believe that it may be more challenging for us to “tune out” because even some our comedies are reminding us of our political turmoil! Otherwise, we are being convinced by ads that we are in some way lacking and should buy something to make ourselves feel better about our selves and the world around us. In other words, shop nonstop because you should be in fear of what may happen and spending keeps you asleep.
          There really isn’t anything new under the sun in all actually, but one has to very vigilant using tight compartments to check where the mind is or there are a whole heck of a lot of places our minds could lead us to which might not always be where we want to go!
          That’s why tight compartments with gratefulness in focus are a good idea. Gratefulness helps us to focus on what’s possible and positive each and everyday in the here and now!
