Published on Feb 1, 2016

I've been getting some requests for a study tip for medical school so I decided to make one to share my favorite medical school life study tips.

1. Active learning: some of my favorite ways to learn actively are reading out loud and rephrase, teaching back to self or to a friend, imagining the information in question format

2. Do application questions early to reinforce: go over "wrong choices" and explain to yourself why they are wrong

3. Use your spare time (audio lectures while doing dishes or laundry or driving): this is especially helpful if you are studying for a very big test like MCAT or USMLE STEP because every minute and every piece of information you can get in your head counts! Try ExamKrackers.com or thinkMCAT.com for some MCAT audio lectures.

4. Use time efficiently; If you're not paying attention, stop studying and take a break for 20-30 minutes

5. Study group: come up with mnemonics together, implement "active learning" tools with each other

6. Make a study schedule: assign certain topics to master each day, divide up the day by resources (Pathoma, flashcards, lecture notes)

Hope you guys find these helpful! Feel free to share some of your favorite study tips!
