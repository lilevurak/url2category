Use this GPA Calculator to:

  • Calculate what your current semester and cumulative grade point averages will be
  • See if  you can achieve a higher GPA with the current number of credits that you have left to complete.

View GPA Calculator

How do I use the GPA Calculator?

First, Run your DARS report on e-services so that you have the numbers that you need to enter into the GPA calculator

How to Calculate your Cumulative and Major GPA using the top half calculator:

  1. Find the Requirement under the University Graduation Requirements that shows your cumulative GPA or the requirement under your Major GPA found toward the end of your DARS
  2. Enter your Current GPA
  3. Enter your GPA credits as your currently completed credits. (GPA does not include any courses taken as CR/NC or Pass/Fail)
  4. Enter grades that you speculate you are going to get for courses that do not have any grades
  5. Click on calculate
  6. The new semester and cumulative GPA you would get for your self-reported grades appears
  7. Click on clear form for recalculations

How to Calculate if you can achieve a higher GPA with the current number of credits that you have left using the bottom half calculator:

  1. Find the Requirement under the University Graduation Requirements that shows your cumulative GPA or the requirement under your Major GPA found toward the end of your DARS
  2. Enter your Current GPA
  3. Figure out how many credits you have left to complete
  4. Enter your total GPA points
  5. Enter the GPA you would like to achieve
  6. Click on calculate
  7. A number less than 4.0 indicates you could achieve that GPA within that number of credits
  8. A number greater than 4.0 indicates that it is not possible to achieve your desired GPA within that number of credits

The calculator uses the following GPA Point system:

  • A+    4.0
  • A      4.0
  • A-     3.67
  • B+    3.33
  • B      3.0
  • B-     2.67
  • C+    2.33
  • C      2.0
  • C-     1.67
  • D+    1.33
  • D      1.0
  • D-      0.67
  • F       0.0
