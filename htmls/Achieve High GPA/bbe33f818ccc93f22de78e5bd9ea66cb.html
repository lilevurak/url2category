How can someone achieve a GPA 4.0 in Engineering?

Here are a few tips.  I maintained a 4.0 for almost all my years of chemical engineering by following these strategies.

1) stay on top of things.  Don't wait until the last minute to do your assignments.  This is not to maintain quality assignments but to thoroughly understand the concepts.  Some things take awhile to sink in.

2)  draw everything out.  Never skip this step.  Many problems seem simple but have a small variance that complicates the solution.  You don't want to miss details.  Also, get strategic about your diagrams.

3) do not skip math steps.  Get in the habit of writing out every single math operation.  Engineering math is not about shortcuts (like business math) - be methodical

4) on that note, do not ignore math shortcomings.  You need to correct lack of knowledge in whatever math you need (i.e differential equations) before it bites you in a test

5) think holistically and in systems.  In engineering, most topics are not discrete.  Topics share similarities in concepts  (most are based in newtonian laws).  To solve complicated problems, you will need to access knowledge from multiple areas.  Do not limit your knowledge to the chapter you're studying.

6) practice common sense.  Before you even start a problem, you should have an intuitive feel for what the end result will look like.  Always question an answer that seems unreasonable. 

In fact, once I knew my final answer was off on a test but did not have time to figure out the issue in my calculation.  On the test I wrote "this answer doesn't seem right.  It should be around this much.  I don't have time to recalculate."  I earned quite a bit extra credit just for that answer.

7)  on that note, write as you solve.  I would explain step by step what I was doing.  This demonstrates mastery over the concepts to your professor.

8) you get what you give, and more.  Help others.  Teach.  You will learn the most by helping and teaching your fellow classmates.

Answered 119w ago
As in any field,
  • study hard (read all of the assigned and recommended texts; do all of the homework; do additional practice problems to prepare for exams)
  • follow instructions (do every assignment as written, not as you think it sould be, and turn it in on time to the exact location specified)
  • don't procrastinate
  • do talk to your professors and TA's
  • don't take classes in fields you aren't good at. (Personally, I'd rather take a wider range of classes and have a non-perfect GPA, but some people have different priorities.)
Also, pray.
