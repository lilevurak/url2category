Re: HOW TO GET A GOOD GPA IN POLY?

by Teacher Aaron » Sat Mar 15, 2014 1:17 pm

There is only one rule: you have to be consistently hardworking.

The reason is because unlike secondary school, you can't slack away for the first couple of years, work hard in your last year, and then you will end up with stellar grades. In polytechnics (and also university), there are no final exams after a few years of study. Each course/module you take contributes to your final GPA score. Screw up even one semester, and you pretty much can kiss a good final GPA score goodbye.

As for how to consistently do well, you have to attend every class, finish all your work, regularly consult your lecturers and tutors to be sure that you are on the right track and for modules that involve project work, be sure to have a bunch of reliable friends as group mates.

Re: HOW TO GET A GOOD GPA IN POLY?

by alng » Sat Mar 22, 2014 2:44 pm

Polys are having modular system, just like universities. In each semester, the students take a few modules. In each module, there are projects, mid term assessments and final exam (for most modules). Each assessment component of each module in the three year poly program will add up to the final and cumulative GPA.

Consistency is very important. In a 15-week (for example) semester, if one slacks for 5 weeks, 1/3 of the semester is gone! Unlike O and A levels, where students can chiong in the last nine months leading to the exam, such strategy does not work at all in the polys and universities.

From day one, one needs to be consistent. Pay attention in class, pre-read, post reflection on learning, complete homework and self-study homework, consult teachers when in doubts on a timely basis; do not snowball readings, homework and doubts. Set a good and comfortable pace. So slow and steady as need good stamina for the full three years.

To get into the popular business school of local uni, one needs a GPA of 3.8/4. All the best!
