LAKEWOOD â In the four years leading up to the moment when Ralph Noriega received his diploma from Georgian Court University, the 22-year-old Neptune man gave little thought to the fact that he is among the first to know only the fully co-ed version of this Catholic institution.

Men received diplomas from the school for years, but it was not until 2013 that they were fully integrated into the university community, invited to live in campus dorms and given the opportunity to play on male sports teams.

It is the culmination of years of changes at the once all-girls college.

"I never really thought about it," said Noriega, who earned his diploma in criminal justice from Georgian Court while working as a paid EMT in Middletown and Oakhurst and a special class 1 officer for Bradley Beach police.

Joseph R. Marbach, the university's first male president, noted the important milestone during the university's commencement ceremony on Thursday.

    "With this graduating class, a coeducational student body becomes the norm," he added. "The student experience has been changed forever."

    Yet women still outnumber men here, 3 to 1.

    Georgian Court was founded as an all-women's college in 1924 when the Sisters of Mercy moved their Mount Saint Mary College from North Plainfield to the luxurious former estate of railroad tycoon George Jay Gould.

    But its history as a woman's college would not last. Faced with growing competition for students and a shrinking population of 18-year-olds, Georgian Court officials saw a need to open the institution to as many potential students as possible.
