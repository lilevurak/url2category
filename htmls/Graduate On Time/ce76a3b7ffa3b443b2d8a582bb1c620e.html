Careers in Early Childhood Education

What is early childhood education?

The field of early childhood education deals with teaching and education young children, usually at a preschool level. Early childhood education emerged as a separate branch of education after several studies were conducted and indicated that the years before a child goes to kindergarten are the most critical for developing their learning abilities and social skills. These studies were conducted by the Department of Education, and several other government organizations in different countries. The increasing awareness about the importance of education during these formative years has led to a special emphasis in educating children in a manner that ensures optimum intellectual, physical, emotional and social development.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics May 2015 statistics, preschool, primary, secondary, and special education school teachers earned a median salary of $53,860. The job growth in this field is expected to be 6% (2014-24). While Preschool teachers earned $28,570 and the job growth in this field is expected to be 7% (2014-24).

In a recently conducted research, The Department of Education determined that the children who received some form of early childhood education outperformed the students who did not obtain any formal education at a young age. The National Research Center on Early Childhood Education has also released data suggesting that initiating the education process at a young age helps kick-start the learning process and allows children to learn vital social skills which will benefit them for the rest of their lives. As such, early childhood education is extremely important in order to ensure that your child grows up to be a successful, healthy and happy individual.

What does the future hold for early childhood education majors?

Thanks to the increasing awareness about the importance of early childhood education through the media, parents around the country are enrolling their kids in special pre-school and kindergarten programs. The Bureau of Labor Statistics estimates that enrollment figures in pre-schools have risen by about 43% in the past two decades, consequently resulting in a tremendous increase in the demand for qualified early childhood education teachers and specialists. The employment opportunities in this field are expected to increase by around 17% to 25% from 2010 to 2010 as stated by the BLS. This in itself is enough to give you a fair idea about the future prospects for early childhood education majors. Most positions in this field also pay better than other positions in education, with wages for preschool teachers between $17,000 and $46,000 per year as according to the US Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS). It further states that the wages and benefits tend to increase with qualification, experience and job type, such as in the case of of kindergarten teachers that earn between $31,000 and $76,000 annually.
What are some of the career opportunities?

Early childhood education graduates have a wide number of career paths to choose from. After graduating with a bachelors degree some people give the teaching licensure exam, which opens up countless opportunities at public and private schools, pre-schools, kindergartens and day care centers. Others who graduate with a Masters degree normally obtain a position as a school or educational program administrator. They also have the choice of continuing their education, and carrying out research work in early childhood education in order to address the critical issues in this field. After three to four years of research work, students submit their thesis to professors for approval, and defend their work and findings at a public dissertation. Thereafter, they are awarded they PhD or Doctoral degree of Early Childhood Education, which opens up countless opportunities in the academic and research sector at colleges, universities and research organizations throughout the world.
