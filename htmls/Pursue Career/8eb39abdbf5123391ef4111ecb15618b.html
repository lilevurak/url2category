Hmm I would say not only skills but also areas you should become familiar.Techniques of Influence and persuasion. Understand how do we (consumers or mere mortals behave) very often unconsciously or noticing were been driven to a trap. There are 2 books that I have as a reference in this field:

How to win friends and influence people / Dale Carnegie

Influence (and persuasion) / Robert Cialdini)

Than, communication skills.

You need as well to be well aware of the market, what is cooking, what is fading, the product lifecycles, the market needs.A crucial thing to do thou, is to interact with your sales team, your production folks, your project teams. Understanding what customers want, demand, pains they suffer or features they wait (feedback from the sales team) can help you to interact with project and production folks to see feasibility. Very often those departments that should be linked and working hand in hand, are almost separate islands, each one thinking is too important than the other and in the end theres no real benefit or not at the excel level from joining their efforts and work together driving the market instead having the market driving you. You must be somehow a visionary (but with your feet on the ground). Remember what Henry Ford said about asking people what would they want. If I asked people what they wanted, they would say faster horses. Instead, he build the famous Ford T.

First off, you have the right idea in two regards: first that youre identifying marketing as an important thing to learn and second that youre trying to learn via skill rather than platform (as far too many do).

In Reverse Tides Marketing Learning Path, we identify the important skills being persuasion (far and away most important), pure sales, pricing, and analytics. And then we advocate specializing within a sub-topic like copywriting, design, media, content, or by channel.

Use that learning path to your advantage for the best sources for self-learning, the right subjects and why you should learn each, how to get experience, and then building qualifications into a winning job application.

Econ and marketing skills are a cool combination. Best of luck!

Observation, Listening and Speaking.

I think these are the three most important skills must for anyone studying marketing. Marketing is about customers psychology. To read their minds, you should be able to observe people. You should listen more stories directly from people. The more you deal with people the more you can think from diverse perspective.
