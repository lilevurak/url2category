How do you find out your career path? How do you find out the thing you are really interested in?

Update Cancel

Connie M. Castellano, 15 years in higher education; 1 year as instructor of oral communication
Answered 44w ago

Sometimes it is challenging to find your career path. Think about the things you are interested in. Are they things that could be career / revenue-building opportunities? Have you taken certain subjects in high school or in a liberal arts-related field in college that you seem to excel at or that you are passionate about? Do you like the Arts - museums? drawing? sculpture? dance? music? Are you interested in numbers - statistics? accounting? Or, do you prefer subjects about history? science? language?

You have to define those things that make you happy or that you want to know more about. Talk with a career counselor to see what areas of employment could come from your interests. Participate in job-shadow or internship experiences because these types of activities help to engage you in a profession so that you can determine if it is a career path that you wish to pursue or not.

It may take some time - but you have to put in the time and research to determine what it is that you really want to accomplish in your career-goals. Good luck to you!
