The Secret Formula To Finding A Job You Love

Olivia Adams06.2014

Share this Post

Image Credit: Businessman holding paper image from Bigstock.

Wouldn’t life be perfect if you could wave a magic wand and immediately have a job you love?

This is a wish many Millennials would love to have granted, but it’s not always possible to achieve. Finding a job you love can sometimes be a life-long journey, but there are some steps you can take to get closer to the dream you want to live.

Although there isn’t a magic formula for immediately landing a job you love, there is a secret formula you can follow. You better grab your pen and paper because this one is worth using throughout your career:

Your Intuition + Experience = Finding a Job You Love

Seems fairly elementary, right? This formula can be very effective for young job seekers when used properly. However, many Millennials usually miss one of the key variables of the formula, which is why it’s difficult to find a job you love.

Finding a job you love means listening to your mind and heart (aka intuition) and going after the experience you need to get one step closer to finding the perfect opportunity. However, if you don’t put all the variables of the equation together, you might as well stop going after finding a job you love.

Now, if you’re serious about finding the perfect job that meets your career aspirations, let’s break down this formula. Each variable will be explained in depth to help you gain exactly what you need to find a job you love.

How to find your intuition:

Finding your intuition will help you discover what you want out of your career. If you’re feeling lost during your search for the perfect job, here are some steps to following your intuition:

1. Ask yourself questions about the next five years, and write down the first answer that comes to mind.

The first step of figuring out what you want out of a job is asking yourself questions about your goals and desires. These questions can be anything about your life goals or career goals, but they should be questions that get you thinking about your future. To get you started, here are some questions to ask yourself to determine where you should begin your search for the perfect job:

  • “Where do I see myself in the next five years?”
  • “What type of income do I want?”
  • “What type of work would make me happy?”
  • “Do I want to move across the country for a job?”

Here comes the tough part of this exercise. When you ask yourself a question, make sure you write down the first thing that comes to mind. Your first response is your intuition telling you what you think is the best answer.

After you ask yourself a series of questions and write down your answers, sit on the list for a few days. When you revisit your list, take a look at your answers and see which ones truly match your career goals. By completing this exercise, you’ll gain more confidence in your goals and figure out how to pave your career path.

2. Make a list of what you want and don’t want out of a job.

This step of finding your intuition is crucial to finding a job you love. The most important thing you can do for yourself throughout your job search is to figure out what you do and don’t want out of the job.

Believe it or not, the smallest details can make or break a job. For example, if you want to find a job where your commute is less than 30 minutes, then chances are you wouldn’t want to have a job where you had to commute an hour and a half each day.

As you make a list of what you do and don’t want out of a job, think of everything from the type of projects you want to have to the type of atmosphere you want to work in. This will guide to employers who offer exactly what you want out of your career.

3. Create your vision statement. 

Once you know your goals and what you want out of a job, it’s time to put everything into a single big picture. Your vision statement is essential to your your job search because it will keep you focused on your goals.

Your vision statement will play a key role in your job search because it will guide you toward the best opportunities for your career. For example, if your vision is to find a job where you can earn a promotion in the next five years, look for jobs that provide you with that opportunity.

4. Finally, trust your instincts. 

It can be difficult to depend on your career goals, especially when you feel like you’re heading down a shaky career path. When you add in the pressure of the accumulating interest on your student loans and your parents trying to get you out of the house, it can be difficult to trust your instincts when it comes to finding a job.

Unfortunately, because of the pressures many post-grads experience during their job search, they snag the first job they can get without thinking it through. For instance, let’s say your first job offer required you to sign a two-year contract. Out of desperation to earn money, you accept the position. Once you start the job, you find that you enjoy most of the work you’re assigned, but after six months, your dream job walks into your life. But now, because of that contract, you’re stuck in a position for another year and a half.

When you begin receiving job offers, you need to accept the position that’s the best opportunity for your career and personal goals. Sometimes turning down a not-so-good job offer can give you much more reward in your career. The only way you can determine which path is best for you is if you trust your instincts.

How to find experience:

As a young job seeker, you need to find a way to prove yourself to employers. The only way you’re going to do this is if you gain relevant experience that will help you land on the radar of recruiters and hiring managers. Here are some tips for finding experience that will lead you to a job you love:

1. Turn your strengths into side projects.

There’s no denying the fact that you possess strong skills and qualities. It’s just a matter of leveraging your strengths and using them to your advantage as you look for ways to gain experience.

A great way to gain experience during your job search is to invest your time in side projects. For example, consider doing some freelancing or volunteering with a nonprofit. Not only will this help you build your resume and portfolio, but also your initiative will make you stand out to employers.

2. Talk to people who work at jobs you want.

If you’ve figured out what type of job you want to have, talk to people who have those jobs. These professionals can offer you valuable insight on how to launch your career, how to get noticed by employers, and what it takes to reach that ideal job.

You want reach out to professionals by sending out networking emails, following professionals on Twitter, and connecting on LinkedIn. Once you make contact with some people, set up informational interviews and even some job shadows. This is a great way to build your network and even get your foot in the door.

How to add your intuition and experience together:

Now that you’ve figured out how to hone your intuition and gain experience, you need to add them together to find a job you love.

Your intuition will motivate you to chase after your career goals, while your experience will turn your goals into accomplishments. As long as you continue to follow your heart, listen to your mind, build your portfolio, and network with professionals, you’ll be on your way to finding a job you love.
