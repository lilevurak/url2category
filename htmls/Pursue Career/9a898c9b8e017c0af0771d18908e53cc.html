Top 3 Books for Discovering Your Ideal Career Path

by Jeff Sanders — Get free updates of new posts here.

Before I created this blog, I had a website called “Graduated and Clueless” where I wrote about how completely inept I was at figuring out life. The main focus was on career paths for recent college graduates.


Photo Credit: Wonderlane via Compfight cc

Since then, I have made some significant discoveries and found my “sweet spot” with my own career. The books listed below are my top books for discovering your ideal career path.

These are a sampling of some of the best resources I have found and I believe you will love them as much as I do.

Top 3 Career Books


So Good They Can't Ignore You by Cal Newport

The title of this book comes from a quotation by Steve Martin, the famous comedian, artist, and musician.

Cal Newport's analysis of what it takes to succeed is outstanding and his thesis is spot on: you have to become so good that others just can't ignore your talents, skills, and abilities. Read this book first.

Photo Credit: Cal Newport


Strengths Finder 2.0 by Tom Rath

Building on Cal Newport's book, Strengths Finder helps you assess and identify your natural abilities.

Your strengths will certainly span many industries, but knowing what you're good at will give you clarity and focus in the work you do.

Photo Credit: Gallup


Business Secrets of the Trappist Monks by August Turak

Outside of Man's Search for Meaning by Viktor Frankl, this is easily one of the best books I have ever read. If you are an entrepreneur, or just a lost soul looking for a higher calling in the work you do, this book is perfect.

The focus on selflessness is incredibly profound and it will likely create a paradigm shift in your thinking as it did mine.

Photo Credit: August Turak

Start Here

When your career is stalling, or you're feeling lost, the best place to turn is wisdom from others who have already experienced this same bewilderment, but then found a way out.

I have found nothing better than books to reveal ancient “secrets” to spark “Aha!” moments in my mind. These epiphanies almost always cause me to run out and do something creative and effective with my own life.

That's the goal with these recommendations. Read them. Read them again. Highlight them. Take notes in the margins. Then, create an action plan and get to work.

Do you have a favorite career book that has worked for you? Let me know in the comments below.
