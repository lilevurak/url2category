Intro

This engaging and comprehensive workshop is full of actionable strategies your team will want to apply right away! Learn to improve your interoffice communication, hone your team’s professional writing skills and gain useful hacks like the best way to network to build lasting relationships. The hands-on workshop is perfect for enriching the communication skills of millennial employees or people of any age. Everyone will leave the session energized and armed with practical tips they can use immediately. Less talking, more doing! That’s what it’s all about.

 

About Danny

Danny is an award-winning author and speaker who teaches professionals to write, network and speak with poise and clarity. He loves to show people how a well-crafted email or memorable networking conversation can change their career (and company) forever. He is the author of two business books. The newest (June 2017) is "Wait, How Do I Promote My Business?", a collection of 100+ templates for website content, press releases, crowdfunding and more. The first book "Wait, How Do I Write This Email?", is 100+ templates for networking, the job search, and LinkedIn. It's sold in FedEx Office stores nationwide.

What to expect

Everything is hands-on there’s no time to get bored or stare at your phone! I can also weave in my own entrepreneurial journey. I self-published a book (that several publishers turned down) now sold in retailers nationwide (ex: Hudson News, Barnes & Noble and FedEx Office). I share the communication skills I relied upon to “break through” on the national scene as an untested author — strategies applicable to anyone in any industry. It’s all about how we use our words in emails, on the phone and in person.

Topics covered:

  1. Writing LinkedIn profiles
    – Professional headline, profile summary and “Experience” sections.
  2. Interoffice communication
    – The sharpest ways to share information with co-workers.
  3. Writing for sales professionals
    – “Cold-call” emails
    – Weaving storytelling into your sales pitch
    – Editing yourself to “cut the fluff”
  4. Client outreach
    – Emails for relationship building and to offer new products/services.
  5. Communication skills for millennial employees
    – How to write with brevity and speak with older colleagues.
You'll be surprised by

The tips provided! It’s not a boring grammar lesson!

Benefits

Attendees will emerge from the workshop as better writers and more confident networkers. That means they will write smarter emails (which leads to more business and $$$) and developer deeper relationships with potential clients (again, more $$$).

As I often say, “When you write well, you open doors.” It’s often an “a-ha” moment for people when they recognize the power of the words we use either in writing or in conversation.

I love what I do because communication skills are critical. But too often, they’re overlooked or ignored. This stuff drives new business!

Important Information

I give 100% to every workshop and always make sure the experience is authentic to the particular audience. In spring 2017, I did two workshops at Ochsner Health System in New Orleans — one in the AM and one in the PM. After the 1st session, several people went back to their desks and told co-workers, “You have GOT to see this guy!”

I do my homework on the business so my advice relates exactly to the work you do. Nothing cookie-cutter about it!

A projector or screen for visual presentations is required for this workshop.
